The Cost of War

"In war, whichever side may call itself the victor, there are no winners, but all are losers."

- Neville Chamberlain

The idea of war in the modern day is often misrepresented compared to what it truly is. Every person can know that war involves death and is “bad”, but how far does that understanding go? As most people have not experienced war first-hand, their preconceived idea of war has to come from somewhere, which is where the idea of media and news comes in.

"The real is not only what can be reproduced, but what is always already reproduced: that is the hyperreal"

- Jean Baudrillard

The “hyperrealism” of modern day media regarding war means that since most people do not have first-hand experience with war, movies, games, and news headlines represent war more “realistically” in a society. In a way, the glorification of war becomes a bigger reality than actual war.

The problem with this is that it takes away the significance of the tragedies in war, war crimes are becoming desensitized after people hear about them endless times. When people hear the numbers of tragedies in war, they become just that; numbers.

"Since fighting first broke out between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) on 15 April, ACLED records 5,550 events of political violence and more than 15,550 reported fatalities in Sudan." [Source]

The numbers are incomprehesible, they are hard to picture.

The figures above place over sixty-five thousand civilian casualties in Ukraine, Russia, Sudan, and Israel/Palestine

These numbers do not include troops and soldiers.

These numbers do not include the forgotten, lost, and missing.

These numbers do not include the full picture.

And Even So...

"The death of one man is a tragedy. The death of millions is a statistic."

As a society, we should never allow people's lives to become a number on a paper.

On January 29, Hind Rajab had been traveling in a car with her uncle, his wife and their four children, fleeing fighting in northern Gaza, when they came under Israeli fire

- CNN

The world lost families. The world lost sons, daughters, fathers, mothers. People who lived for each other.

The world lost journalists, reporters. People who lived for the truth.

Sudan: Powerhouse U.S. doctor 'killed for nothing'

The world lost doctors, nurses, combat medics, war volunteers. People who lived to help others.

These are just 5 of the 61000 civilians who have been acknowledged as dead. Acknowledged as a number on a count. Disgustingly enough, these 5 are the “lucky ones'' among the victims. These five people were noticed by the media because of their role in society and because they symbolize the losses that so many people feel.

They deserve more than to be headlines on a news article, they deserve the dignity to be recognized as more than a symbol, they deserve to live the life of a regular human being, and as human beings. To the others among the sixty-one thousand people dead in just these conflicts, they deserve to be more than a number on a screen. They deserve the same as the rest of us.

If each were to live just 20 more years.

It means that over 1.2 million years were robbed from all of them.

And since these numbers are underestimates?

The cost of war is much larger.

The cost of war couldn't be repaid in 1 million years.
